Cooking for a Family (and Learning to Love It)

With a family of five, I spend a lot of time in the kitchen—cooking, planning meals, and yes, doing a lot of cleaning up too! Over the past year and a half, I made a big change: I switched to a mostly vegan diet, and I’ve brought my family along for the ride. It’s been a learning experience, but also a really fun one.

Why I Enjoy Cooking

At first, cooking felt like a chore. But now I enjoy it for a few important reasons:

  • I know exactly what I’m eating – No mystery ingredients or surprise additives.
  • I can change recipes to fit my family’s needs – It’s easy to swap ingredients to fit everyone’s tastes and dietary needs.
  • Vegetables are actually really good – When they’re cooked the right way, they’re full of flavor.
  • Eating at home saves money – We still get great meals, without the cost of eating out.
  • Leftovers are amazing – They make busy days so much easier.

Our Favorite Foods

Some of our favorite types of food are Chinese, Italian, and Mexican. The great news is that it’s totally possible to make delicious plant-based versions of all of them! Stir-fries, pastas, tacos, and more—there are so many options that use only plant-based ingredients and still taste great.

Trying New Things (and Getting Kids On Board)

My younger kids don’t always love trying new foods, but we’re getting there one meal at a time. My teenager, though, has been very open to this journey and has even given the thumbs-up to several of our go-to plant-based meals.

Cooking for my family has become something I enjoy and even look forward to. I’m excited to keep exploring new recipes and sharing what I learn along the way!
